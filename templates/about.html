Value of Vote

Canada : Total Population - 35 Mn | Seats - 338

Value of vote determines how important a person’s vote is based on their residence. The baseline (or 100%) is the total population (35 Mn) divided by the number of electoral districts or seats (338) i.e. approximately 1 seat per 100k Canadians . The national average is 100K people per elected candidate and in an electoral district if there are 50k people,then the ‘value’ of a single vote would be 200% of baseline. If, on the other hand, 150k people live in an electoral district, the value of a single vote would be 67% of the baseline. Data from the 2016 Statscan survey is used.
